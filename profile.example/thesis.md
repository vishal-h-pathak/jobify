# Hunting Thesis — Alex Quinn

> Read first by the scorer. This encodes judgment, not facts. When this file
> and other profile prose disagree, this file wins.

## The thesis in one paragraph

Alex is a platform/infrastructure engineer who is happiest building the
systems other engineers build on. Eight years of backend work, increasingly
deep in distributed systems and developer platforms. The ideal next role is a
senior/staff platform or infra position at a product company with real scale
(millions of users or high event throughput) and an engineering culture that
values simple designs, good docs, and sane on-call. Flexible on domain, much
less flexible on engineering quality and ownership.

## Hard constraints (violating any of these = score floor, do not surface)

- **Remote, or hybrid in/near Denver.** Relocation only for an exceptional
  role + comp combination.
- **No pay cut.** Total comp at or above ~$165k; target $175–205k. A
  standout role at the low end is fine; below current comp is not.
- **Real engineering ownership.** Not a ticket-factory or a role where the
  platform decisions are made elsewhere and you only implement.
- **No crypto/Web3 trading products** — not a domain of genuine interest.

## Tiers

**Tier 1 — platform / infrastructure / distributed systems.** Owning
developer-facing platforms, core services, or infrastructure at scale. This
is the center; score generously here.

**Tier 2 — ML / data-platform engineering.** The systems *around* models
(feature stores, serving infra, data pipelines), not the modeling itself.
Strong fit given the backend depth — treat a good Tier 2 match as nearly
Tier 1.

**Tier 3 — generalist senior backend / full-stack at mission-driven product
companies.** Heavily company-dependent. Filter hard: the mission and the team
have to carry it, because the work itself is less differentiated.

## Energy signals (weight JD language against these)

Strong positive — the role day-to-day involves:
- Owning a service or platform end-to-end: design, ship, operate
- Scale that makes the problems interesting (throughput, latency SLOs,
  multi-region, big data volumes)
- Improving developer experience / internal tooling that teams adopt
- A culture signal: strong docs, blameless on-call, design-review rigor

Strong negative — the role day-to-day involves:
- Pure feature factory work; "groom the backlog with stakeholders"
- Maintaining a legacy system with no mandate to improve it
- Interchangeable big-company SWE work with no platform ownership
- Heavy process, little autonomy

## Named companies (calibration anchors — generalize from the *why*)

Admired for engineering culture + platform depth: companies known for strong
internal platforms and public engineering writing (e.g. the kind of place
that publishes detailed infra blog posts). Generalize from the *why*:
product companies at real scale where platform/infra is treated as a
first-class discipline, not a cost center. These names indicate the *shape*
of the target, not an allowlist — verify each posting on its own merits.

## Tone notes for downstream prompts

Alex reads as a pragmatic builder who values simplicity and ownership over
novelty for its own sake. Materials should frame him as a platform engineer
who makes teams faster — never round up to "architect who only designs" or
down to "generalist who closes tickets."
