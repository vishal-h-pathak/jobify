## How He Communicates

- **Clear and unfussy.** Medium-length sentences, plain words. Explains the
  "why" before the "how."
- **User-grounded.** Frames technical choices in terms of the person using the
  UI — "so a keyboard user can…", not "to satisfy the spec."
- **Concrete about craft.** Names the component, the token, the WCAG criterion,
  the adoption number. Avoids hand-wavy "improved UX."
- **Collaborative tone.** Writes like someone who pairs with designers — credits
  the team, frames trade-offs as shared decisions.
- **Quietly opinionated.** Has clear views on API design and accessibility but
  states them as reasoning, not decree.

## What NOT to Do

- No buzzword stacking ("pixel-perfect," "seamless," "best-in-class," "rockstar").
- No inflated verbs ("spearheaded," "revolutionized") where "built" or "led" is true.
- No vague UX claims without a mechanism ("made it more intuitive" — say how).
- No treating accessibility as a buzzword; only claim a11y work that shipped.
- Don't open a cover letter with "I'm passionate about design" — lead with what
  was built and the user problem it solved.

## What TO Do

- Lead with the concrete: the component/system, how it works, who adopted it.
- Tie the role to actual shipped work, not feelings about design.
- Use precise terms (tokens, focus management, ARIA) in plain sentences.
- Be direct about motivation — "I want this role because of X."
- Frame accomplishments as user problems solved and teams unblocked.

## Cover Letter Guidelines

The cover letter should read like Sam explaining to a thoughtful peer why this
specific role fits. Structure:

1. **Opening:** what the team is building and the one concrete thread connecting
   it to Sam's real design-systems / a11y work (not "passion").
2. **Middle:** 2–3 concrete things built or remediated that are directly
   relevant, with enough detail to be credible. Narrative, not bullets.
3. **Close:** why the timing fits and a low-key, direct call to action.

Total length: 3–4 short paragraphs. No filler.

## Resume Guidelines

- Professional experience only — no interests or personal statements.
- Rewrite skill categories to emphasize what's relevant to the target role.
- Bullets should be specific: what was built, with what, and the measurable
  adoption / a11y / performance outcome.
- Reorder sections to lead with the most relevant work for the target role.
- Never fabricate components, tools, audit results, or metrics Sam doesn't have.
