# Hunting Thesis — Sam Rivera

> Read first by the scorer. This encodes judgment, not facts. When this file
> and other profile prose disagree, this file wins.

## The thesis in one paragraph

Sam is a frontend engineer who is happiest building the design system other
product teams build on. Seven years of frontend work, increasingly centered on
component libraries, design tokens, and accessibility. The ideal next role is a
senior design-systems or frontend-platform position at a product company that
treats craft and inclusivity as real engineering concerns. Flexible on product
domain, much less flexible on design culture and accessibility commitment.

## Hard constraints (violating any of these = score floor, do not surface)

- **Remote, or hybrid in/near Austin.** Not relocating.
- **No pay cut.** Total comp at or above ~$145k; target $150–180k.
- **Real frontend craft.** Not a role that treats the UI as an afterthought
  bolted onto a backend, or where design decisions are made elsewhere and you
  only translate mockups pixel-by-pixel.
- **No ad-tech / surveillance-adjacent products** — not a domain of interest.

## Tiers

**Tier 1 — design systems / frontend platform.** Owning a component library,
tokens, and adoption tooling other teams depend on. Score generously here.

**Tier 2 — accessibility-focused frontend.** Roles where a11y is a first-class
mandate. Strong fit given the track record — treat a good Tier 2 as near Tier 1.

**Tier 3 — senior product frontend at mission-driven companies.** Heavily
company-dependent. Filter hard: the mission and the design culture have to carry
it, because the work itself is more generic.

## Energy signals (weight JD language against these)

Strong positive — the role day-to-day involves:
- Owning a design system or component library end-to-end
- Accessibility as a stated, resourced priority (not "nice to have")
- Design-engineering collaboration: tokens, Figma workflows, shared language
- A culture signal: strong docs, design reviews, craft taken seriously

Strong negative — the role day-to-day involves:
- Pixel-pushing to spec with no API or system ownership
- "Full-stack" roles that are 90% backend with frontend as a side duty
- Accessibility treated as a last-minute checkbox
- Heavy process, little design partnership

## Named companies (calibration anchors — generalize from the *why*)

Admired for design-systems maturity and public craft: companies that publish
their component libraries or write seriously about accessibility and design
engineering. Generalize from the *why* — product companies where the design
system is a funded, first-class discipline — not an allowlist. Verify each
posting on its own merits.

## Tone notes for downstream prompts

Sam reads as a craft-driven builder who cares about users and consistency over
novelty. Materials should frame Sam as a design-systems engineer who makes teams
ship faster and more accessibly — never round up to "designer who can't code" or
down to "generic React dev."
